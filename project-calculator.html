Calculator buit from logic gates


In the summer, I become interested about how the most fundamental levels of computer work, so I tried to build a calculator out of logic gates. This calculator can take inputs in the range from 0 to 255 and do the basic four operation.

From this experience, I learn a lot of thing, including how the cpu of a computer works, how does RAM store and get information and a lot more.

Some of the problems

  • since human use decimal and computers use binary, I need to take in decimal, convert it into binary, compute the result and then convert the result back into decimal. Double Dabble algorithm and its reverse version can handle this problem
  • Among the basic four operations, devision is the hardest to implement. I choose to use non-restoring division algorithm and the circuit is just HUGE
  • How the Memory works is also pretty complicated.

Reminder

When you open the file, some of the wire will be red. just click simulation, reset simulation and then enable simulation again. some of the wires will flash, just click clear once and the calculator will function properly.
